What is AI, Really?

AI stands for Artificial Intelligence.

Think of it like this: you show a computer millions of examples of something, and it learns patterns from those examples. Then, when you ask it something new, it uses those patterns to give you an answer.

Simple Example:

Show a computer 1 million pictures of cats. It learns what makes something look like a cat. Then you show it a new picture, and it can tell you if it's a cat or not.

What is ChatGPT?

The simplest way to understand it

ChatGPT in Plain English

Think of ChatGPT like a super smart autocomplete

You know how your phone suggests the next word when you're texting? ChatGPT does that, but for entire conversations.

It read almost everything on the internet (books, articles, websites) and learned patterns about how humans talk, write, and think. So when you ask it a question, it uses those patterns to give you a helpful answer.

What it does:

  • Answers questions
  • Writes emails and essays
  • Helps with coding
  • Explains complex topics
  • Translates languages

What it doesn't do:

  • It doesn't "think" like humans
  • It doesn't remember past conversations (unless you pay)
  • It can make mistakes
  • It doesn't know things after 2023 (in free version)

How ChatGPT Actually Works (Simple Version)

Step 1: Training (Learning)

OpenAI (the company that made ChatGPT) fed it billions of pages of text from the internet. It's like giving a student millions of books to read.

Step 2: Pattern Learning

The AI learned patterns like: "When someone asks 'What is...', they usually want an explanation" or "After 'Hello', people often say 'How are you?'"

Step 3: Responding

When you type a question, ChatGPT looks at all the patterns it learned and predicts what words should come next to give you a helpful answer.

It's not magic - it's math and patterns, but really, really advanced math

Why Computer Chips Matter

Training AI is like doing billions of math problems. You need really powerful computers.

The Problem:

  • Regular computer chips are too slow
  • AI needs special chips called GPUs
  • These chips cost $30,000+ each
  • You need thousands of them

Why This Matters:

Only a few companies can afford to build the biggest AI models. That's why you see the same names over and over: OpenAI, Google, Microsoft.

How Do They Make Money?

Different ways to charge for AI

4 Ways AI Companies Make Money

Most Common

1. Pay Per Use (API)

Developers pay a small fee every time they use the AI. Like paying per text message.

Examples: OpenAI charges $0.01 per 1,000 words

Good for: Apps that want to add AI features

2. Monthly Subscription

Pay $20/month for unlimited use. Like Netflix for AI.

Examples: ChatGPT Plus, Claude Pro

Good for: People who use AI a lot

3. Enterprise Deals

Big companies pay millions for special versions with privacy, support, and guarantees.

Examples: Microsoft Copilot for Business, Anthropic Enterprise

Good for: Large companies that need reliability

4. Free + Paid Services

Give away the AI for free, but charge for hosting it, customizing it, or supporting it.

Examples: Meta's Llama (free), but companies pay to host it

Good for: Building a community first

Why Microsoft is Winning

They didn't build the best AI, but they're making the most money from it

1. Smart Partnership

Instead of building their own AI, they invested $13 billion in OpenAI. Now they get to use the best AI.

Lesson: Sometimes partnering is smarter than building

2. Put It Everywhere

They added AI to Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Windows - everywhere their customers already work.

Lesson: Distribution beats innovation

3. Charge for It

They charge $30 per person per month for AI features in Office. With 365 million users, that adds up fast.

Lesson: Existing customers = easy revenue

The Big Idea

Microsoft proved you don't need the best AI - you need the best way to get AI to people who will pay for it.

What You Should Remember

1

AI is pattern matching, not thinking

It learns from examples and predicts what comes next. It's very good at this, but it's not "alive" or conscious.

2

Building big AI is expensive

Only a few companies can afford to train the biggest models. That's why you see the same names everywhere.

3

Getting AI to users matters more than having the best AI

Microsoft proved this - they didn't build the best AI, but they put it where millions of people already work.

4

There are multiple ways to make money from AI

Pay per use, subscriptions, enterprise deals, or free models with paid services. Each works for different customers.
